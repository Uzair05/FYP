FYP Project - AI Model for Vehicle Identification and Tracking

Project Information

Background

Missing Motor Vehicles is a growing problem in urban cities that causes several billions of dollars worth of damages [1].
As of current most, law enforcement departments in the world rely on traditional means of finding and tracking down missing vehicles. These methods range from manually checking the license plates of each vehicles in traffic stops and parking lots to relying on tips from the community [1].
This project aims to create a system to automate the process of finding missing vehicles and alerting law enforcement.

Research Gap

One of the means of tracking missing vehicles is by watching surveillance footage of street cameras to try and track suspicious vehicles for clues. But this is a very time-consuming and labor-intensive approach.
As of current, we do not have a reliable system to automate the process of watching surveillance footage. That is why this project aims at creating a system to watch surveillance footage and trigger an alert whenever it manages to find a reported missing vehicle.

Objective

The project aims to create a modular machine learning model that can watch surveillance footage
